Patent US10570778B2 Granted Feb 25, 2020
Coupling System for Turbocharger and Emission Control Device
Bhageerath Bogi, Daniel Jakobcic, Sami Siddiqui, Rodolfo Palma Sr., Xiankai Song · Assignee: Ford Global Technologies LLC · Filed: Sep 11, 2017
Abstract
Methods and systems are provided for a coupling system of a turbocharger and emissions control device of a vehicle. The coupling system includes chamfered surfaces on one or more flanges of the turbine housing and catalyst housing, reducing the physical distance between the two components. By routing a fastener through angled passages in the chamfered flanges, the design minimizes the gap between the turbocharger exit and the catalyst face — directly reducing thermal losses in the exhaust stream during cold-start conditions. This approach improves the enthalpy delivered to the catalyst at engine start, enabling faster catalyst light-off and supporting compliance with stringent SULEV 30 / Tier 3 Bin 30 emissions standards in turbocharged engine platforms.

Process to Optimize Exhaust Enthalpy (POEE) for Cold-Start Emissions Compliance
Bhageerath Bogi et al. · Assignee: Ford Motor Company · Developed at Altair Product Design Inc. (Client: Ford Motor Company)
Summary
Invented and patented an advanced simulation methodology — Process to Optimize Exhaust Enthalpy (POEE) — for modeling the thermal behavior of exhaust hardware components from the exhaust port to the catalyst brick face. The methodology introduces "total sensible enthalpy available at the catalyst face" as a predictive metric for successful catalyst light-off during cold-start conditions in turbocharged engines. This CAE framework was adopted by Ford to assess SULEV 30 / Tier 3 Bin 30 compliance across multiple vehicle programs. The work was recognized as a Top 5 CAE project globally at Ford (2015) and earned the Ford Chief Award for Innovation. The companion publication (SAE 2017-01-0141) documents the full methodology.

Journal Paper SAE International · 2017 2017-01-0141
Exhaust System Thermal Management: A Process to Optimize Exhaust Enthalpy for Cold Start Emissions Reduction
Ray Host, Peter Moilanen, Marcus Fried, Bhageerath Bogi · Ford Motor Company · WCX 17: SAE World Congress Experience · Published March 28, 2017
Abstract
Future North American emissions standards (Tier 3 Bin 30 / LEVIII SULEV 30) require the exhaust catalyst to exceed 80% conversion efficiency within 20 seconds of engine start. Turbocharged engines face unique challenges as the turbocharger significantly increases heat dissipation in the exhaust system, delaying catalyst light-off. This paper presents a CAE methodology to assess the thermal performance of exhaust hardware components from the exhaust port through to the catalyst brick face, and introduces the concept of "total sensible enthalpy available at the catalyst face" as a predictive metric for successful catalyst light-off during cold-start conditions. The framework enables engineers to evaluate design trade-offs and guide exhaust system architecture decisions that support compliance with next-generation emissions regulations across turbocharged engine platforms.

The Role of Dynamic Pricing Models in Increasing Marketplace Profitability
Bhageerath Bogi · International Journal of Scientific Research in Computer Science, Engineering and Information Technology · Sep–Oct 2024 · pp. 988–1002
Abstract
This paper examines the theoretical frameworks and real-world applications of dynamic pricing models in digital marketplaces, analyzing how companies leverage real-time market signals to optimize profitability at scale. It explores demand elasticity modeling, competitor price monitoring, and inventory-driven pricing strategies — drawing on production experience with multi-retailer platforms serving millions of SKUs. The paper evaluates gradient-boosted and regression-based elasticity models alongside reinforcement learning approaches, and discusses implementation trade-offs in latency- sensitive pricing pipelines. Key findings highlight the compounding impact of real-time markdown optimization and surge pricing on gross margin, with case evidence of sustained profitability improvements across seasonal demand cycles.

Bhageerath Bogi · International Journal of Computer Science and Technology · 2024 · Article P10
Abstract

Drop-Off Prediction Models and Route Optimization for Cost-Effective Delivery Operations
Bhageerath Bogi · ResearchGate · Published December 2024
Abstract
This paper investigates the integration of drop-off prediction models with route optimization approaches to improve the cost-efficacy of last-mile delivery operations. Drawing on production systems processing 400,000+ packages weekly, it presents a gradient-boosted drop-off prediction model deployed within a mission-critical pay-computation service — achieving an 8% reduction in shopper pay costs through improved flight-plan estimates. The paper explores deep learning architectures and evolutionary algorithms for joint optimization of drop-off probability and route assignment, and evaluates trade-offs between model complexity, inference latency, and operational accuracy under SLA constraints. Results demonstrate that accurate drop-off forecasting, when tightly coupled with real-time route matching, delivers substantial cost savings at scale in gig-economy delivery platforms.
